### 2. **Clinical Leadership (ER Medical Director, Nursing Lead, Quality)**

**Stakeholders:** ER physicians, nurse manager, patient safety officer

**Key Concerns:**
- Patient safety & quality (will dispatch system make errors?)
- Clinician workflow impact (will it help or hurt their day?)
- Autonomy & clinical judgment (am I losing control to a computer?)

**Message Template:**
> *"This is a queuing system, not a clinical decision system. You still make all care decisions. What changes: instead of asking 'who's next?' or waiting for a page, you see a real-time list. **Touchpoints:**
- **Week 1 (Pre-Launch):** 90-min working session: review dispatch rules, design edge cases, confirm safety protocols
- **Week 2 (Pre-Launch):** 60-min clinical education session on system design
- **Week 3 (Launch):** Bedside champions available during first day of go-live
- **Daily (Pilot):** 15-min clinical huddle (feedback, tweaks, safety check-in)
- **Weekly:** Steering committee (you're on it, helping drive decisions)

**Deliverables:**
- Dispatch rules flowchart (clinical validation sign-off)
- Safety protocol document
- System demo video (5 min, showing actual use)
- Weekly reflection template (what's working, what's hard?)

## FAQ Document (For All Stakeholders)

### General Questions

**Q: Why are we changing things? Isn't ER already busy?**
A: ER is busy *because of bottlenecks*, not because we're at capacity. Data shows doctors are only 50% utilized during peak wait times. That means patients aren't reaching doctors fast enough. This fixes the routing, not the workload.

**Q: How long will this take to show results?**
A: We'll see improvements within 2â€“3 weeks of the pilot launch. Full benefits after 8â€“12 weeks.

**Q: Will this make my job harder?**
A: No. It removes manual coordination work (asking "who's next?" paging). You'll see your queue in real-time and spend more time with patients, less time coordinating.

---

### Clinical Questions

**Q: Does the computer make clinical decisions?**
A: No. The system suggests the next patient; you decide if that's right. If you see a different priority, you override. Clinical judgment stays with you 100%.

**Q: What if the queue system fails?**
A: We have a fallback. If the system goes down, we revert to manual coordination (what we do today). No impact on patient care.

**Q: How do I know it's safe?**
A: The dispatch rules were designed by your clinical team. Patient safety is rule #1. We have a safety officer reviewing every decision and weekly safety huddles.

---

### Staff Adoption Questions

**Q: Do I have to change how I work?**
A: Some workflow changes: checking queue board instead of asking people, accepting/overriding system suggestions instead of manual assignment. But your core clinical work stays the same.

**Q: What if I don't feel comfortable with the new system?**
A: Training is mandatory but thorough. You'll get hands-on practice before go-live, a quick-ref card at your desk, and support during the first week. Ask questions anytime.

**Q: Will I be judged if I struggle with the system?**
A: No. Pilot is a learning phase. We'll iterate based on your feedback. There's no penalty for strugglingâ€”that's why we pilot.

---

### Leadership Questions

**Q: What's the financial upside?**
A: $5â€“6M annually from 25â€“35% throughput improvement (40â€“58 additional patients/day). Investment is $840K; payback is <3 weeks.

**Q: What's the risk?**
A: Pilot risk is low (12 weeks, one shift, revert easily). Main risk is staff adoption (mitigated by training + change mgmt). Financial risk is minimal (payback fast).

**Q: When do we scale?**
A: Only after pilot succeeds (KPIs + safety + adoption targets met). Full rollout takes 8â€“12 more weeks (phased by shift).
