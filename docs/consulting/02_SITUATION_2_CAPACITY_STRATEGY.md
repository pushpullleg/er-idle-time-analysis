## ROOT CAUSE: Why Day Shift is Overloaded

### Analysis: Patient Distribution Across Shifts

| Shift | Patients | % of Total | Hours | Patients/Hour |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DAY** | 9,792 | 65.3% | 8 | **13.6** |
| **EVENING** | 2,986 | 19.9% | 8 | 4.2 |
| **NIGHT** | 2,222 | 14.8% | 8 | 3.1 |

### The Root:
**65% of ALL daily patient volume concentrates in ONE 8-hour period.** This isn't an even distribution—it's a massive peak.

### Why Does This Happen?

**Patient Behavior Pattern (Natural):**
- Emergency rooms see peak volume during waking hours
- Most people seek care during day
- Fewer emergencies happen during night
- This is universal, not unique to this hospital

**Your Staffing Model:**
- Day: 3.5 doctors (planned for capacity)
- Evening: 3.5 doctors (same as day—why?)
- Night: 1.5 doctors (appropriately lower)

**The Problem:**
You're staffing evening the same as day, but evening gets only 19.9% of volume. This creates the paradox:
- Evening: 47% utilization (too many doctors)
- Day: 154% utilization (too few doctors)
- **These are connected—you have capacity, it's just in the wrong shift**

## SITUATION 2: WHY OPTIMIZATION ALONE FAILS

**The Process Optimization Limit:**

Process improvements (Situation 1) can get you to 60-70% utilization by:
- Eliminating workflow delays
- Better patient-doctor matching
- Reducing idle moments
- Optimizing transitions

But then you hit a **hard ceiling: the math.**

```
Even with PERFECT workflow:
- 13.6 patients/hour DEMAND
- 8.75 capacity with 3.5 doctors
- Gap = 4.85 patients/hour CANNOT WAIT ANYWHERE

They're either:
1. Served (requires more doctors)
2. Diverted (to urgent care, telehealth, competitor)
3. Waiting (patient satisfaction drops)
4. Left without being seen (legal/safety risk)

You CANNOT process improvements away from this gap.
```

### The Consultant's Observation:

Process optimization is a **multiplier** on existing capacity, not a replacement for insufficient capacity.

```
Current: 50% efficiency × 8.75 capacity = 4.4 patients/hour actually served
With Phase 1: 60% efficiency × 8.75 capacity = 5.3 patients/hour
With Phase 2: 70% efficiency × 8.75 capacity = 6.1 patients/hour

But demand is 13.6 patients/hour.
Gap remains: 13.6 - 6.1 = 7.5 patients/hour STILL UNSERVED.

Process improvements alone cannot bridge a 7.5 patient/hour gap.
You need MORE CAPACITY (doctors).
```

## SITUATION 2: THE STAFFING STRATEGY

### Option A: Increase Day Shift Doctors

**Current State:**
- Day: 3.5 doctors
- Demand: 13.6 patients/hour
- Needed capacity: 13.6 ÷ 2.5 = **5.4 doctors**
- Gap: 5.4 - 3.5 = **1.9 doctors needed**

**Realistic Implementation:**
- Add 1.5 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 7.25) × 100 = 187% (STILL broken)
- Add 2 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 8.75) × 100 = 155% (STILL broken)
- Add 2.5 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 10.0) × 100 = 136% (Still problematic)
- Add 3 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 10.5) × 100 = 129% (Still problematic)
- Add 3.5 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 11.25) × 100 = 121% (Approaching limits)
- Add 4 FTE → New utilization = (13.6 ÷ 12.0) × 100 = 113% (Acceptable with optimization)

**Consultant Recommendation for Option A:**
Add 1-2 FTE to day shift morning hours (7 AM-12 PM specifically), where 36.5% of bottlenecks occur.

### Option B: Redistribute Evening Doctors to Day

**Current State:**
- Evening: 3.5 doctors, 4.2 patients/hour → 47% utilization (excess capacity)
- Day: 3.5 doctors, 13.6 patients/hour → 154% utilization (crisis)

**Possible Reallocation:**
- Move 1 doctor from evening to day
- Evening: 2.5 doctors, 4.2 patients/hour → 67% utilization (still acceptable)
- Day: 4.5 doctors, 13.6 patients/hour → 120% utilization (approaching acceptable)

**Consultant Assessment of Option B:**
- Lower cost (no new hiring)
- May cause evening issues (still 32.7% bottleneck rate)
- Not recommended as standalone (addresses day, breaks evening)
- Could be PART of hybrid solution

### Option C: Hybrid Staffing Model

**The Smarter Approach:**

Combine:
1. **Shift-specific staffing** (don't staff all shifts the same)
2. **Early morning intervention** (add 1 doctor at 5 AM-12 PM window)
3. **Peak-hour flexibility** (additional doctor 7 AM-1 PM specifically)
4. **Evening optimization** (slight reduction acceptable given 47% baseline)

**Resulting Model:**
```
CURRENT:
- Day: 3.5 doctors (7 AM-3 PM)
- Evening: 3.5 doctors (3 PM-11 PM)
- Night: 1.5 doctors (11 PM-7 AM)

PROPOSED:
- Night: 1.5 doctors (11 PM-7 AM) [unchanged]
- Pre-morning: 1 additional doctor (5 AM-7 AM) [new—addresses transition]
- Morning peak: +1.5 doctors (7 AM-12 PM) [targeted where volume peaks]
- Afternoon: 2 doctors (12 PM-3 PM) [day tapering down]
- Evening: 3 doctors (3 PM-11 PM) [slight reduction, still 54% utilization]

Result:
- Total FTE impact: +1.5 net (not +4 flat hires)
- Targets the specific problem (morning 7-12 PM is 36.5% of bottlenecks)
- Leaves evening with headroom
- Night unchanged
```
