EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Emergency Department Operations Optimization
================================================================================

ANALYSIS PERIOD: 2025-01-01 to 2025-03-31
FACILITY: MC_ER_EAST
TOTAL VISITS ANALYZED: 15,000

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. OPERATIONAL INEFFICIENCY DETECTED
   • 2,240 instances (14.9% of visits) where doctors were idle 
     while patients waited in queue after triage completion
   • Average wait time during inefficient periods: 38.1 minutes
   • Total patient hours lost to inefficiency: 1,423 hours

2. FINANCIAL IMPACT
   • Current capacity utilization: 85.1%
   • Potential revenue opportunity: $1,067,362 annually
   • Estimated additional patient capacity: 712 visits/year
   • ROI on optimization initiative: 2035%
   • Payback period: 0.6 months

3. PATIENT SATISFACTION CORRELATION
   • Patients experiencing idle-doctor delays: 3.59/5.0 avg satisfaction
   • All other patients: 3.57/5.0 avg satisfaction
   • Satisfaction gap: -0.02 points
   • Estimated satisfaction improvement potential: -0.6%

4. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
   Primary Drivers:
   • Lack of real-time queue visibility for physicians
   • Manual patient-doctor assignment process
   • Inefficient shift handoff procedures
   • Suboptimal triage-to-doctor workflow
   
   Most Affected:
   • Shift: EVENING (880 incidents)
   • Peak Hours: [7, 16, 8]
   • Avg idle doctors during incidents: 2.8
